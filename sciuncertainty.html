Communicating Scientific Uncertainty

Our interdisciplinary research group combines expertise from Earth and environmental sciences, data visualization and transdisciplinary course development and teaching to explore the communication of scientific uncertainty through research, teaching and public engagement activities. Below are summaries of our main projects:

Making uncertainties visibles: New approaches to uncertainty visualization

The project focuses on improving communication from experts to experts and from experts to nonexperts with regard to scientific uncertainties associated with earth and environmental data, models and predictions. We do this by applying different methods to sample datasets to quantify uncertainties. We then compare, evaluate and improve these methods for communication with experts and non-experts using different approaches such as eye-tracking, decision-making tasks and survey questionnaires. The knwoledge gained will be used in the development of a learning module on the topic on scientific uncertainty for teaching and learning at universities. This project was awarded the Dr. K.H. Eberle Prize in 2024

Communicating uncertain science to the Public Audiences

I use terrestrial remote sensing (LiDAR) to detect and monitor rockfall activity in deglaciated alpine valleys located in the Swiss Alps. Using terrestrial laser scans data, I calculate rockwall retreat rates under present-day conditions and their temporal change since deglaciation.

For more information on this topic please see:

Mohadjer, S., Ehlers, T.A., Nettesheim, M., Ott, M.B., Glotzbach, C., and Drews, R., 2020. Temporal variations in rockfall and rockwall retreat rates in a deglaciated valley over the last 11 ka. Geology, v. 48 (Download PDF)

Dietze, M., Mohadjer, S., Turowski, J. M., Ehlers, T. A., and Hovius, N., 2017. Seismic monitoring of small alpine rockfalls - validity, precision and limitations, Earth Surf. Dynam. 5, 653-668. (Download PDF)
