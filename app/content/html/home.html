My research interests lie primarily in the understanding of mountain hazards, particularly earthquakes and rockfalls, and the quantification of hazards using a variety of different techniques including remote sensing, geographic information systems, and high precision LiDAR mapping of topography. She is also interested in bridging the communication gap that exists between the scientific community and the general public. Current research topics include:

  • Crustal deformation processes using GPS geodesy
  • Quantifying mountain hazards using terrestrial remote sensing (Lidar)
  • Database management system for scientific analysis and applications (e.g., Central Asia Active Fault Database
  • Geohazards research, education and community outreach (e.g., ParsQuake Project
